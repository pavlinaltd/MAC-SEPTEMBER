...Leaving their wives and youngsters behind and seeking quick riches in the new land, they took off by the hundreds, knowing that what they would earn in America in a few years, would last them a lifetime back home. Most of them would eventually return to their families, with their life savings, but robbed of their youth and now strangers to their already grown children...

The theme of immigration and immigrant life has been present in the shaping of America and has remained a powerful reality in today’s American consciousness, Partings is another link in the story of American immigrant life.

COLORFUL CRIB

Stoyan's father wove a basket
Made of pine straws, brightly colored
And placed it by the fire
So his mother can lull her first born
Baby Stoyan, their little new born.
Sleep my darling son
In the warmth of your pretty crib
Your mother will sing to you,
Your father, will lull you
We’ll both watch over you…..
We’ll both watch you grow.

In remote Macedonian villages, mothers and grandmothers sung acapela lullabies which were then reinterpreted by artists worldwide, who by injecting a taste of their own musical heritage in the new arrangements.
